DeepSeek V2 (May 2024) is the model that introduced [MLA](/llm-arch-kb/attention/mla/) and
established the DeepSeek architectural lineage. The four defining choices:

- **MLA attention**: latent-space K, V compression to a per-token bottleneck ($d_c = 512$),
  with a decoupled RoPE head ($d_R = 64$). 5–7× smaller KV cache than MHA on this scale.
- **[DeepSeekMoE](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/deepseek-moe/)**: 162 routed experts plus 2 shared
  experts per MoE layer, top-6 routing. Established the fine-grained-plus-shared design
  pattern that V3 inherits.
- **Standard auxiliary balance loss**: V2 still uses Switch-style aux loss for MoE load
  balancing. V3 later replaced this with the
  [aux-loss-free scheme](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/aux-loss-free/).
- **YaRN context extension**: trained at 4K base, extended to 128K via
  [YaRN](/llm-arch-kb/positional/yarn/) post-pretraining.

The 60 layers and Pre-Norm RMSNorm + SwiGLU stack are otherwise canonical. The cumulative
effect of MLA + fine-grained MoE was a sparse-activation model with quality comparable to
dense 70B models at ~1/3 the inference cost.

V2 is included here as the *original* MLA + DeepSeekMoE adopter; for the latest in the lineage,
see [DeepSeek V3](/llm-arch-kb/models/deepseek-v3/).
