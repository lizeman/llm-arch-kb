<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The cost of softmax is the cost of materializing $N \times N$

Standard attention forms $S = Q K^\top / \sqrt{d_h} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times T}$ explicitly,
because softmax is a row-wise nonlinearity that does not commute with matrix multiplication.
The quadratic-in-$T$ work is in that one matrix. For decoder inference at a context length
$T = 100\text{K}$, each new token's attention reads $T$ prior K vectors and $T$ prior V vectors
from cache — bytes read scale linearly per token, total scale quadratically over the
generation.

Katharopoulos et al. (2020, §3) frame this as a kernel-method problem. Any associative
similarity function $\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k})$ defines attention via

$$\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{q}_i)_n = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^T \mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}_i, \mathbf{k}_j) \mathbf{v}_j}{\sum_{j=1}^T \mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}_i, \mathbf{k}_j)},$$

and softmax is the choice $\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}) = e^{\mathbf{q}^\top \mathbf{k} / \sqrt{d_h}}$.
If instead $\mathrm{sim}$ is *decomposable* — writable as
$\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}) = \phi(\mathbf{q})^\top \phi(\mathbf{k})$ for some feature
map $\phi$ — then the sums over $j$ no longer couple to the per-query $i$, and the matrix
multiplication can be reordered to do the $j$-summation once and reuse it for every query.
This is the entire content of the paper, expanded over §3.1–§3.3.

The savings are asymptotic: $O(T^2 d_h)$ becomes $O(T d_h^2)$ for the prefix-sum form, and the
autoregressive decode becomes a constant-state recurrence — see §2.

Lineage. The 2020 paper is the cleanest exposition of an idea with a longer history: Tsai et
al. (2019, "Transformer Dissection") introduced the kernel-formulation framing;
Wang et al. (2020, "Linformer", [arXiv 2006.04768](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.04768)) tried
projecting $K, V$ to a fixed rank; Choromanski et al. (2020, "Performer",
[arXiv 2009.14794](https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.14794)) followed Katharopoulos with a
random-feature approximation of softmax. The descendant production tracks branch in two
directions: state-space-model variants (S4, Mamba, Mamba-2) and gated-delta variants
(RetNet, DeltaNet, Gated DeltaNet, Lightning Attention).

Preview: linear attention buys $O(T)$ training compute and $O(1)$ per-token decode at the cost
of giving up softmax's sharpening, with the empirical penalty showing up at long-context
recall.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · Derivation</p>
## Reordering the matmul; the recurrent form

Start from the unnormalized attention output for query $i$, dropping the $1/\sqrt{d_h}$ scale
for notational clarity (it gets folded into $\phi$):

$$y_i = \sum_{j=1}^T \mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}_i, \mathbf{k}_j)\,\mathbf{v}_j.$$

Substitute the kernel decomposition $\mathrm{sim}(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}) = \phi(\mathbf{q})^\top \phi(\mathbf{k})$
with $\phi: \mathbb{R}^{d_h} \to \mathbb{R}^{d'}_{\ge 0}$ (non-negativity is required so the
denominator stays positive):

$$y_i = \sum_{j=1}^T \phi(\mathbf{q}_i)^\top \phi(\mathbf{k}_j)\,\mathbf{v}_j = \phi(\mathbf{q}_i)^\top \underbrace{\sum_{j=1}^T \phi(\mathbf{k}_j) \mathbf{v}_j^\top}_{=: S \,\in\, \mathbb{R}^{d' \times d_h}}.$$

The sum no longer depends on $i$. Define the **state matrix**

$$S = \sum_{j=1}^T \phi(\mathbf{k}_j)\,\mathbf{v}_j^\top \in \mathbb{R}^{d' \times d_h}, \qquad z = \sum_{j=1}^T \phi(\mathbf{k}_j) \in \mathbb{R}^{d'},$$

and the normalized attention is

$$\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{q}_i) = \frac{\phi(\mathbf{q}_i)^\top S}{\phi(\mathbf{q}_i)^\top z}.$$

The denominator $\phi(\mathbf{q}_i)^\top z$ is the linear-attention analog of softmax's
$\sum_j e^{\mathbf{q}^\top \mathbf{k}_j}$ — the partition function — and is what keeps the
output bounded.

**Symbol legend.** $\mathbf{q}_i, \mathbf{k}_j \in \mathbb{R}^{d_h}$ — query and key vectors;
$\mathbf{v}_j \in \mathbb{R}^{d_h}$ — value vector; $\phi$ — element-wise feature map (the
paper uses $\phi(\mathbf{x}) = \mathrm{elu}(\mathbf{x}) + 1$ for non-negativity, §3.4); $d'$ —
feature dimension, $\ge d_h$ in some variants and $= d_h$ in the paper's experiments; $S, z$ —
the running state, total size $d'(d_h + 1)$ floats.

**Compute cost.** Forming $S$ and $z$ requires $T$ rank-one updates, each at $O(d' \cdot d_h)$,
so the prefix-sum cost is $O(T \cdot d' \cdot d_h)$ — **linear in $T$**, vs softmax attention's
$O(T^2 \cdot d_h)$. At $d' = d_h = 128$ and $T = 100\text{K}$: linear attention does
$\sim 1.6 \cdot 10^9$ ops per layer, softmax does $\sim 1.3 \cdot 10^{12}$. Three orders of
magnitude, all in the absence of the $T \times T$ score matrix.

**Causal recurrence.** For autoregressive decoding, the prefix sums need to update
incrementally: when token $t$ arrives, the state increments by

$$S_t = S_{t-1} + \phi(\mathbf{k}_t)\,\mathbf{v}_t^\top,\qquad z_t = z_{t-1} + \phi(\mathbf{k}_t),$$

and the output is

$$\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{q}_t) = \frac{\phi(\mathbf{q}_t)^\top S_t}{\phi(\mathbf{q}_t)^\top z_t}.$$

This is the paper's "Transformers are RNNs" claim (§3.3): the autoregressive variant is
literally a recurrent neural network with hidden state $(S_t, z_t) \in \mathbb{R}^{d' \times
d_h} \oplus \mathbb{R}^{d'}$. The state is **independent of $T$** — a single matrix of size
$d' \cdot d_h$ floats. Per-token decode cost is $O(d' \cdot d_h)$, vs softmax attention's
$O(T \cdot d_h)$ growing with context.

**Choice of $\phi$.** Why elu+1 rather than, say, ReLU or softplus? The paper's §3.4
notes three requirements: non-negativity (denominator), differentiability (gradients), and
expressivity (the inner product $\phi(\mathbf{q})^\top \phi(\mathbf{k})$ must vary enough to
discriminate keys). ReLU fails on the second when activations clamp to zero; softmax fails on
the first if you do not exponentiate. Subsequent work (Performer's positive random features,
Hedgehog's learned $\phi$) tried richer feature maps; the consistent finding is that no
fixed $\phi$ recovers softmax's sharpening on long-range recall tasks.

**Geometric reading.** Softmax produces near-one-hot attention when one $\mathbf{q}^\top
\mathbf{k}_j$ dominates: the distribution concentrates exponentially. Linear attention with
any bounded $\phi$ produces a fundamentally smoother distribution; the weights $\phi(\mathbf{q})^\top
\phi(\mathbf{k}_j)/\phi(\mathbf{q})^\top z$ sum to 1 but cannot concentrate sharper than the
inner-product geometry allows. This is the structural reason linear attention loses on
needle-in-haystack tasks: the layer cannot *isolate* a single key.

**Capacity of the state.** $S \in \mathbb{R}^{d' \times d_h}$ holds at most $d' \cdot d_h$
scalar parameters of information about the past. After $\sim d'$ key-value pairs of high
rank, new writes overwrite old ones — a property analogous to associative memory's
catastrophic interference (Schlag et al. 2021,
[arXiv 2102.11174](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.11174)). This is the empirical reason
fixed-state linear attention's long-context recall degrades.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Reference implementation</p>
## Sketch

```python
def linear_attention(Q, K, V, phi):
    # Q, K: [B, T, d_h]   V: [B, T, d_v]
    # phi: feature map, e.g. lambda x: F.elu(x) + 1.
    phi_Q = phi(Q)                            # [B, T, d']
    phi_K = phi(K)                            # [B, T, d']
    # Non-causal: prefix sums collapse to global sums.
    S = einsum("btd,btv->bdv", phi_K, V)      # [B, d', d_v]  ← the state matrix
    z = phi_K.sum(dim=1)                      # [B, d']       ← the normalizer
    num = einsum("btd,bdv->btv", phi_Q, S)    # [B, T, d_v]
    den = einsum("btd,bd->bt", phi_Q, z).unsqueeze(-1)
    return num / (den + 1e-6)

def linear_attention_causal(Q, K, V, phi):
    # Autoregressive form — runs as an RNN; one rank-1 update per step.
    phi_Q = phi(Q); phi_K = phi(K)
    B, T, d_prime = phi_K.shape; d_v = V.shape[-1]
    S = zeros(B, d_prime, d_v); z = zeros(B, d_prime)
    out = empty_like(V)
    for t in range(T):                                    # the recurrence
        S = S + einsum("bd,bv->bdv", phi_K[:, t], V[:, t])  # rank-1 update
        z = z + phi_K[:, t]                                  # vector update
        num = einsum("bd,bdv->bv", phi_Q[:, t], S)
        den = einsum("bd,bd->b", phi_Q[:, t], z).unsqueeze(-1)
        out[:, t] = num / (den + 1e-6)
    return out
```

The mechanical difference vs softmax attention is the missing $\mathrm{softmax}$ call and the
swapped associativity. In softmax attention the score matrix $Q K^\top$ must be built before
the row-wise nonlinearity. Here, $\phi(K)^\top V$ is built first; the per-query work then
becomes a small matrix-vector product against $\phi(\mathbf{q}_i)$.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Empirical evidence</p>
## Where linear attention works, where it loses

Katharopoulos et al. (2020, Table 2) on synthetic copy/sort tasks at $T = 1{,}000$: linear
attention runs $\approx 4000\times$ faster than softmax attention at inference (matching
expected $T = 1\text{K}$ scaling), and within 5% perplexity on the copy task. On
character-level Wikitext-103 (Table 4), linear attention at $L = 16$ achieves 25.6 BPC vs
softmax's 25.3 — a small gap. Long-range arena tasks (Tay et al. 2021,
[arXiv 2011.04006](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.04006), Table 1) report linear-transformer at
50.5% average accuracy vs full-attention's 59.4% — the gap widens on the longest tasks
(ListOps, Pathfinder-X) where sharp position-specific attention is required.

Independent reproductions at LM-scale. Schlag et al. (2021, "Linear Transformers Are
Secretly Fast Weight Programmers", [arXiv 2102.11174](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.11174)) show
that the recurrent state's rank-1 update is mathematically the fast-weight programming rule
of Schmidhuber (1992), and that adding a **delta update** ($S_t = S_{t-1} - \beta \phi(\mathbf{k}_t)
\phi(\mathbf{k}_t)^\top S_{t-1} + \phi(\mathbf{k}_t) \mathbf{v}_t^\top$) improves WikiText
perplexity by $\sim 0.4$ over vanilla linear attention. This is the trick that the 2024–25
DeltaNet / Gated DeltaNet / Kimi Delta Attention lineage builds on.

The state-space-model successor track. Mamba (Gu & Dao 2023,
[arXiv 2312.00752](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00752), §4.4) and Mamba-2 (Dao & Gu 2024,
[arXiv 2405.21060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.21060)) recast the linear-attention recurrence as
a selective state-space model, and at 1.4B parameters Mamba beats Pythia-1.4B on the Pile by
$\approx 1$ perplexity. The Mamba-2 paper explicitly identifies SSMs and linear attention as
"the same thing under different parameterizations" (§3) — the linear-attention framing wins
at clarifying the duality.

Hybrid architectures are the production reality. Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B
(Qwen team 2025, [HF model card](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct))
runs Gated DeltaNet on 36 of 48 layers, interleaved 3:1 with full-attention "Gated Attention"
layers — 75% of layers are linear-cost, 25% are softmax. The model card reports that the
hybrid recovers full-attention quality on standard benchmarks while saving $\approx 60\%$ of
prefill compute at 32K context. Kimi Linear (Moonshot AI 2025,
[arXiv 2510.26692](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26692), Table 4) reports a similar 3:1 mix with
its Kimi Delta Attention variant at 48B parameters, matching MLA-only baselines on
mathematical reasoning while running $\approx 2\times$ faster at 128K context. Nemotron-3-Nano-30B
(NVIDIA 2025) ships a hybrid Mamba-2 / softmax stack at 23/52 SSM layers and reports
comparable behavior. The pattern is consistent: a minority of softmax layers — interleaved,
not stacked — recover the recall and sharpening that pure linear-attention loses.

A pure-linear frontier-scale decoder has yet to publicly match softmax-attention quality at
the same compute budget. RetNet (Sun et al. 2023,
[arXiv 2307.08621](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.08621), Table 2) gets within $\approx 0.5$
perplexity of softmax Transformer at 7B params — close but not parity. The hybrid pattern is
where the lineage has gained production traction; the original pure-linear formulation of
Katharopoulos et al. has not, in 2024–26.

<Summary>
**Linear Attention in one breath.** Replace softmax with a decomposable kernel $\phi(\mathbf{q})^\top
\phi(\mathbf{k})$; reorder the matmul so the $j$-summation is done once; autoregressive decode
becomes an RNN with a $(d' \times d_h)$ fixed-size state. Linear in $T$ training, $O(1)$
per-token decode. Loses softmax's sharpening and bounded recall capacity; the production
descendant track (DeltaNet, Gated DeltaNet, Mamba) is hybrid with softmax, not pure linear.
</Summary>
