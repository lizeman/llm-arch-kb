<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## Why KV cache is the bottleneck

Attention's KV cache is the dominant inference-time memory cost in long-context generation.
At each new token, every prior key and value vector for every layer must be in memory. For a
typical 64-head model at 128 head-dim that is 16 KB per token per layer in fp16, or about 1 GB
at 64K context for a 30-layer model — comparable to the parameters themselves.

Group-Query Attention (GQA) reduces this by letting groups of query heads share a single K, V
pair. Multi-Query Attention (MQA) is the extreme: one K, V for all heads. Both trade KV memory
for some quality. MLA changes the shape of the tradeoff.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The compression</p>
## Down-project to a latent

MLA replaces per-head K, V projections with a down-projection to a small latent vector
$\mathbf{c}^{KV}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_c}$ per token:

$$\mathbf{c}^{KV}_t = W_{DKV}\,\mathbf{h}_t,\quad d_c \ll H \cdot d_h.$$

The latent is what gets cached. At attention time, we up-project to per-head K and V:

$$\mathbf{k}^{(i)}_t = W^{(i)}_{UK}\,\mathbf{c}^{KV}_t,\quad \mathbf{v}^{(i)}_t = W^{(i)}_{UV}\,\mathbf{c}^{KV}_t.$$

The compression ratio is the cache savings. On DeepSeek-V2, $H d_h = 16384$ while $d_c = 512$ — a
32× ratio at the projection level (a smaller ratio at the cache level once decoupled RoPE is added).

```python
# Down-project K,V to a single latent vector
c_kv = x @ W_DKV                       # [B, T, d_c]

# At attention time, up-project (typically absorbed into W_Q at inference)
k = c_kv @ W_UK                         # [B, T, H, d_h]
v = c_kv @ W_UV

# Decoupled RoPE head carries position (see §3)
k_rope = rope(x @ W_KR)                 # [B, T, d_R]
k = concat([k, k_rope], dim=-1)
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Decoupled RoPE</p>
## Why RoPE doesn't fit in the latent

RoPE rotates per-head K vectors before attention. If we apply RoPE *after* the up-projection,
the rotation matrix has to be applied at every attention step — which kills the inference
trick of absorbing $W_{UK}$ into $W_Q$.

If we apply RoPE *before* compression, the rotation entangles the down-projection with position
in a way that can't be inverted at inference. MLA solves this by adding a small decoupled RoPE
head: a single position-aware K head of dimension $d_R$ (typically 64) concatenated with the
reconstructed per-head K.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

On DeepSeek-V2's reported ablations (Table 9), MLA outperforms MHA on BBH and MMLU while
cutting KV cache 5.5–6.5×; GQA on their setup underperforms MHA. DeepSeek-V3 keeps the same MLA
configuration across 61 layers.

The two costs are: (a) an extra rotary head adds slight overhead per layer; (b) implementing MLA
efficiently in a kernel is harder than GQA, since you have to fuse the up-projection with the
attention compute to avoid materializing the per-head K, V.

<Summary>
**MLA in one breath.** Compress K and V to a per-token latent; reconstruct heads at attention time.
5–7× smaller cache than MHA, on-par or better quality. Costs: an extra rotary head, harder to
implement than GQA. Seen in: DeepSeek V2 / V3.
</Summary>
