<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The cost MHA pays at inference

At training time, MHA's per-head K and V projections are essentially free — they parallelize
across the batch and sequence dimensions. At inference time, with batch size 1 and autoregressive
generation, the KV cache becomes the dominant memory cost. Each token's prior K and V vectors
must stay resident; each head needs its own copy.

For a 64-head, 128 head-dim, 80-layer model at 128K context, the cache pushes 300 GB — far
beyond what a single GPU holds. Long-context decoder inference is bottlenecked here.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The simplification</p>
## One write-head

Shazeer's 2019 proposal: keep $H$ query heads, but project K and V only *once* per layer. All
heads attend over the same K, V pair.

$$\mathbf{Q}_i = X W_Q^{(i)} \quad (i = 1, \ldots, H),\qquad \mathbf{K} = X W_K,\quad \mathbf{V} = X W_V.$$

The KV cache per token per layer drops from $2 \cdot H \cdot d_h$ to $2 \cdot d_h$ floats — an
$H\times$ reduction. For the 64-head, 128 head-dim, 80-layer setup, the cache goes from 300 GB
to under 5 GB at 128K context.

```python
def mqa(x, W_Q_all, W_K, W_V, W_O, H, mask=None):
    B, T, D = x.shape
    d_h = D // H
    q = (x @ W_Q_all).view(B, T, H, d_h).transpose(1, 2)  # [B, H, T, d_h]
    k = (x @ W_K).unsqueeze(1)                            # [B, 1, T, d_h]
    v = (x @ W_V).unsqueeze(1)
    logits = (q @ k.transpose(-2, -1)) / d_h**0.5         # broadcasts over H
    if mask is not None: logits = logits.masked_fill(mask, float("-inf"))
    out = logits.softmax(-1) @ v                          # [B, H, T, d_h]
    return out.transpose(1, 2).reshape(B, T, D) @ W_O.T
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · The quality cost</p>
## Why nobody runs pure MQA anymore

Shazeer's paper reports MQA approximately matching MHA on translation tasks, with a 12× decode
speedup on a 6-layer model (Table 2). PaLM (Chowdhery et al. 2022) used MQA at 540B and reported
no measurable quality difference at that scale.

Later work was less sanguine. The GQA paper (Ainslie et al. 2023) found MQA losing ~1.5
quality points vs MHA on summarization and translation at the T5-XXL scale (Table 1). The
asymmetry is intuitive: every head must now agree on which positions matter, where MHA let
heads specialize on different relations.

The pattern that emerged: MQA's savings are too valuable to leave on the table, but its quality
cost is real enough to motivate a middle ground — GQA's partial sharing across small groups.
GQA closes most of the quality gap while keeping ~$H/8$-fold cache reduction. Today every
production decoder we document uses GQA or MLA, not plain MQA.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · MQA's place in the lineage</p>
## Conceptual stepping stone

MQA's importance is not as a final destination but as the move that proved KV sharing was
viable. Once a single shared K, V worked at all, the design space opened: group sharing (GQA),
latent compression (MLA), and decoupled positional sub-heads (MLA's RoPE band) all build on the
basic observation that per-head K, V was over-provisioned.

For context on the descendants:

- [GQA](/llm-arch-kb/attention/gqa/) — partial sharing across groups of query heads.
- [MLA](/llm-arch-kb/attention/mla/) — latent-space compression instead of head sharing.

**MQA in one breath.** Share one K, V across all H query heads. H× KV cache reduction; small but

modern KV-friendly attention, but no current frontier open decoder ships pure MQA.
