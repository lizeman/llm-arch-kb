<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The auxiliary-loss tax

Every prior MoE in this knowledge base — Switch, GShard, Mixtral, DeepSeekMoE V2 — uses an
auxiliary load-balancing loss with a coefficient $\alpha \sim 0.01$ added to the training
objective:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{LM}} + \alpha \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{aux}}.$$

The auxiliary term penalizes deviation from uniform expert utilization. It works — experts
stay balanced — but it has a cost: the gradient signal from $\mathcal{L}_{\text{aux}}$ pulls
the gate away from what it would otherwise choose. Ablations (DeepSeek V3 Table 5) show this
cost as a measurable but small quality regression vs an ideal gate.

DeepSeek V3's question: can you get load balance without the gradient interference?

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The bias</p>
## Add a bias at selection; not at output

Maintain a learnable scalar bias $b_i \in \mathbb{R}$ per expert $i$. At every step, the gate
computes logits $g(\mathbf{x})$, *adds the bias* for the top-K selection, but uses the
*unbiased* logits for the output weighting:

$$i^* = \mathrm{topK}\bigl(g(\mathbf{x}) + b\bigr),\quad \mathrm{out}(\mathbf{x}) = \sum_{i \in i^*} \mathrm{softmax}\bigl(g(\mathbf{x})\bigr)_i \cdot f_i(\mathbf{x}).$$

The bias only matters at the top-K argmax. Once experts are selected, the final mixing weights
come from the original gate logits. The model's *expressive output* is unaffected by the bias;
only the *routing decision* is.

The bias is updated after every step based on the realized expert utilization:

$$b_i \leftarrow b_i + \gamma \cdot \bigl(\bar{f} - f_i\bigr),$$

where $f_i$ is the fraction of tokens routed to expert $i$ in the batch and $\bar{f} = 1/E$ is
the target uniform utilization. The update rate $\gamma$ is small (DeepSeek uses $10^{-3}$).
Over-used experts get a more negative bias and become less likely to be selected; under-used
experts become more likely.

```python
def aux_loss_free_routing(x, gate, experts, biases, K, gamma=1e-3):
    logits = gate(x)                                   # [B, T, E]
    biased = logits + biases[None, None, :]
    top_idx = biased.topk(K, dim=-1).indices
    # Output weights from UNbiased logits — restricted to selected experts
    weights = logits.softmax(-1).gather(-1, top_idx)
    out = dispatch_and_combine(x, experts, top_idx, weights.softmax(-1))
    # Update biases based on realized utilization (post-hoc, not in graph)
    realized = utilization_from_topk(top_idx, num_experts=biases.shape[0])
    biases.add_(gamma * (1.0 / biases.shape[0] - realized))
    return out
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Why it doesn't hurt quality</p>
## Selection vs expression

The mechanism's correctness rests on a clean separation: the bias affects *which* experts
process the token (a categorical, non-differentiable decision) but not *how much* their
outputs count in the residual stream. The gate's softmax over the selected K experts is
computed from the original logits, so the model's downstream signal is identical to what an
unbiased gate would have produced — given the same selection.

If the gate would have routed to experts $\{A, B\}$ for token $t$, the auxiliary-loss
approach pulls the gate toward routing $t$ elsewhere to balance utilization, and downstream
expression suffers. The bias approach instead routes $t$ to $\{A, B\}$ *and* shifts a *later*
token to a different pair to compensate. Total utilization balances; per-token expression
stays optimal.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

DeepSeek V3 (§3.4 in the paper) reports the aux-loss-free scheme achieving comparable
utilization variance to the standard aux loss across 256 routed experts × 61 layers, with
0.2–0.4 perplexity-point improvement on most evaluation benchmarks. The improvement is small
in absolute terms but stable across ablations and consistent across model sizes.

The scheme has been adopted by subsequent DeepSeek releases (V3.1, V3.2). Other MoE families
(Mixtral, Kimi, OLMoE) currently use the original auxiliary loss; whether they switch will
likely depend on whether the small quality bump is reproducible at non-DeepSeek scales.

improvement; production-deployed in DeepSeek V3.
