<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The GLU-variant family

Shazeer's 2020 paper introduced a family of gated bilinear FFNs of the form

$$\mathrm{FFN}(\mathbf{x}) = W_2\bigl((W\mathbf{x}) \odot \phi(V\mathbf{x})\bigr),$$

where $\phi$ is the gating activation. The variants studied in the paper:

- **ReGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{ReLU}$.
- **GeGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{GELU}$.
- **SwiGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{Swish}$, where $\mathrm{Swish}(z) = z \cdot \sigma(z)$.

All three improve on the vanilla ReLU FFN by a similar margin. GeGLU and SwiGLU are the two
descendants that survived into production decoders; ReGLU and the original GLU (sigmoid gate)
are rarely seen post-2020.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The activation</p>
## GELU vs Swish

GELU is the Gaussian Error Linear Unit:

$$\mathrm{GELU}(z) = z \cdot \Phi(z),$$

where $\Phi$ is the standard normal CDF. In practice it is approximated as

$$\mathrm{GELU}(z) \approx z \cdot \sigma(1.702 \cdot z),$$

which differs from $\mathrm{Swish}(z) = z \cdot \sigma(z)$ only in the temperature of the inner
sigmoid. GELU saturates more sharply for large $|z|$; Swish is smoother.

```python
def geglu_ffn(x, W, V, W2):
    return F.gelu(x @ V.T) * (x @ W.T) @ W2.T
```

The temperature-1.702 GELU and the temperature-1 Swish both pass enough information through
the gate at the input regimes seen in transformer training that their final-model qualities
differ by less than typical seed noise.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Why the field split</p>
## Historical lineage

GeGLU and SwiGLU emerged from the same paper, but two roughly parallel research lineages
adopted one or the other and never switched:

- **Google → Gemma → Gemini-derivatives**: GeGLU.
- **Meta → Llama → DeepSeek → OLMo**: SwiGLU.

The reason is mostly path-dependence. PaLM (Google, 2022) used SwiGLU; then the Gemma team
moved to GeGLU for Gemma 1, evaluated SwiGLU during ablations, found no difference, and stayed
with GeGLU. Llama 1 used SwiGLU and the entire downstream family inherited it.

Neither paper makes a strong claim that one beats the other; Shazeer (2020, Table 1) reports
them within 0.05 perplexity points on T5-scale pretraining.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · The 8/3 rule still applies</p>
## Parameter accounting

GeGLU has the same three-projection structure as SwiGLU, so the convention $d_{\text{ff}} =
(8/3)\,d_{\text{model}}$ used to keep parameter count matched against a ReLU FFN applies
unchanged. See the [SwiGLU entry](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/swiglu/) for the derivation.
