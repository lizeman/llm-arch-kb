<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## What 384 experts buys

[DeepSeekMoE](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/deepseek-moe/) established the **shared-expert** pattern at
the open frontier — one always-on expert handles the common-knowledge load while many
fine-grained routed experts specialize. DeepSeek V3 ran this with 256 + 1 routed/shared
experts and top-8 routing. Kimi K2 (Moonshot AI, July 2025) widens the routed-expert pool to
**384** — the largest publicly documented routed-expert count in an open release as of
mid-2025.

The motivation is the same as DeepSeekMoE's: more, smaller experts give finer specialization
than fewer, larger experts. The cost is more aggressive routing imbalance and a larger gate
that has to learn to pick well from 384 options. K2's contribution is the engineering needed
to make this train stably at 1T total parameters.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The configuration</p>
## Layout per public sources

From the model card and tech report:

- **Total parameters:** 1T
- **Active parameters per token:** 32B
- **Routed experts per MoE layer:** 384
- **Shared experts per MoE layer:** 1 (always on)
- **Routing:** top-K with K small relative to 384 (the exact K is on the smaller side per the
  released config; see the Hugging Face model card for the precise value at release)
- **Attention:** [MLA](/llm-arch-kb/attention/mla/)-style latent compression, same family as
  DeepSeek V2 / V3
- **Other stack:** Pre-Norm RMSNorm, SwiGLU FFN inside each expert

The active-to-total ratio is 32 / 1000 ≈ 3.2%, comparable to DeepSeek V3's 37 / 671 ≈ 5.5% but
on the sparser side.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · MuonClip — the training-stability lever</p>
## What's actually new

The architecture itself is in the same family as DeepSeekMoE. K2's distinctive contribution is
on the optimization side: the **MuonClip** optimizer, a member of the Muon family (Jordan et
al. 2024 line of work on matrix-aware second-order updates) augmented with weight clipping for
trillion-parameter-scale stability.

The Moonshot team's writeup attributes a meaningful share of K2's training-cost efficiency to
this optimizer choice — they report that the MoE balance and routing dynamics that historically
required careful aux-loss tuning at 100B+ scale stayed in line with weaker regularization once
MuonClip was used.

This is an MoE-adjacent (not strictly architectural) detail, but it's the load-bearing
difference between K2's ability to scale to 384 experts and prior open MoE attempts at similar
expert counts.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Where it sits in the lineage</p>
## DeepSeekMoE's wider sibling

K2 doesn't introduce a new MoE primitive — the shared-expert layout, the latent KV cache, the
SwiGLU expert FFN are all established choices. What it demonstrates is that those choices
**scale**: the recipe that DeepSeek V2 ran at 236B total scales cleanly to K2's 1T total when
combined with a more aggressive expert count and a stability-tuned optimizer.

The technique-level question K2 leaves open: does the gain from 384 experts vs 256 actually
come from the wider gate, or from MuonClip alone? The released configuration ties them
together, and Moonshot has not published the ablation.

For the model itself see [Kimi K2](/llm-arch-kb/models/kimi-k2/). For the lineage that K2's
MoE structure inherits from, see [DeepSeekMoE](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/deepseek-moe/) and
[aux-loss-free routing](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/aux-loss-free/).
