<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## What 384 experts buys at 1T total

The MoE granularity question — "how many experts of what size?" — moved twice between 2024
and 2025. Mixtral 8x7B (Jiang et al. 2024) shipped the coarse end: $E = 8$ full-FFN experts,
top-2, no shared lane. DeepSeekMoE (Dai et al. 2024,
[arXiv 2401.06066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.06066)) shipped the next step in: split each
FFN into $m$ fine-grained slices, keep activated parameters fixed, and reserve $K_s$ always-on
shared experts to absorb the common-knowledge load that would otherwise be redundantly
duplicated across every routed expert (Dai et al. 2024, §2.2). DeepSeek V3 productionized this
at $E = 256$ routed + 1 shared with top-8 routing across 671B total / 37B active (DeepSeek-AI
2024, [arXiv 2412.19437](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.19437), §3.2).

Kimi K2 (Moonshot AI, July 2025,
[arXiv 2507.20534](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.20534)) takes the next-but-not-obvious step:
**widen the routed pool to $E = 384$** while keeping the rest of the DeepSeekMoE shape
(1 shared expert, top-8 routing, SwiGLU experts, MLA attention) and scale to 1T total / 32B
active. The active-to-total ratio of 32 / 1000 = 3.2% is sparser than DeepSeek V3's 37 / 671 =
5.5%; the expert count is the largest publicly documented in any open release at the spec's
verified date. The fine-grained-plus-shared design ground that K2 inherits is summarized at
[Sparse MoE](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/sparse-moe/) and
[DeepSeekMoE](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/deepseek-moe/); this entry focuses on what K2 changes.

The architectural delta from V3 is small. The training-stability delta is large: K2's
distinctive technical contribution is the **MuonClip** optimizer — a member of the Muon family
(Jordan et al. 2024 line on matrix-aware second-order updates) extended with logit and weight
clipping for trillion-parameter MoE stability. Moonshot's writeup attributes the wider expert
count's viability primarily to this optimizer choice rather than to the routing-mechanic
changes (Moonshot AI 2025, §3.1).

The contribution in one sentence: scale fine-grained shared-expert MoE to $E = 384$ at 1T total
parameters, demonstrating that the DeepSeekMoE recipe survives the next size class when paired
with the right optimizer.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · Derivation</p>
## What changes from DeepSeek V3

The MoE layer's functional form is the DeepSeekMoE form, unchanged. For input $\mathbf{x} \in
\mathbb{R}^{d_{model}}$, $E_r = 384$ routed experts $\{f_1, \dots, f_{E_r}\}$ and $E_s = 1$
shared expert $f^{(s)}$, top-$K$ routing with $K = 8$:

$$\mathrm{MoE}(\mathbf{x}) = \underbrace{f^{(s)}(\mathbf{x})}_{\text{always on}} + \sum_{i \in \mathcal{T}_K(\mathbf{x})} g_i(\mathbf{x}) \cdot f_i(\mathbf{x}),$$

with $\mathcal{T}_K(\mathbf{x}) = \mathrm{Top}_K\bigl(\sigma(\ell(\mathbf{x})) + b\bigr)$ — the
top-$K$ of the *biased* gate, in the aux-loss-free style of DeepSeek V3 §3.4 — and the gate
weights $g_i$ taken from the *unbiased* gate restricted to the selected set:

$$g_i(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{\sigma(\ell_i(\mathbf{x}))}{\sum_{j \in \mathcal{T}_K(\mathbf{x})} \sigma(\ell_j(\mathbf{x}))},\quad i \in \mathcal{T}_K(\mathbf{x}).$$

The gate's nonlinearity is sigmoid $\sigma$ (per-expert independent), not softmax — same as
DeepSeek V3's "sigmoid gate, grouped top-K" form (DeepSeek-AI 2024, §3.2). For the bias-update
mechanics that keep loads balanced without an auxiliary loss term, see
[aux-loss-free routing](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/aux-loss-free/).

**Why widen from 256 to 384?** DeepSeekMoE's §4.2 ablation shows quality at fixed activated
parameters increases monotonically with the routed-expert count $E_r$ from $E_r = 16$ through
$E_r = 64$, with diminishing returns past $E_r = 64$. The Moonshot team's bet is that the
diminishing-returns asymptote was not yet reached at $E_r = 256$, so a further 50% widening
extracts additional specialization. The release does not include the controlled
matched-activated-parameters ablation that would isolate this gain from MuonClip.

**Why the MoE recipe needs MuonClip.** Adam-style training of $E = 384$ MoE at 1T parameters
runs into two coupled instabilities at the gate. (1) **Logit explosion**: under Adam the QK
logits in the attention modules and the gate logits in the MoE modules can drift toward large
values during long pretraining runs, producing saturating softmax/sigmoid and effectively dead
gate dimensions (Moonshot AI 2025, §3.1; also Dehghani et al. 2023 on QK-norm). (2)
**Optimization conditioning**: Muon (Jordan et al. 2024) is a matrix-aware second-order
optimizer that uses orthogonal-projection-based updates, which empirically condition the gate
weights better than Adam — at the cost of slightly more memory per step.

MuonClip combines Muon with two clip layers. Let $W \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ be a weight
matrix at training step $t$ with Muon update $\Delta W$. The MuonClip step is

$$\widetilde{W}_t = W_{t-1} + \Delta W,\quad W_t = \frac{\widetilde{W}_t}{\max(1,\ \lVert \widetilde{W}_t \rVert / \tau)},$$

where $\lVert \cdot \rVert$ is the spectral (operator) norm and $\tau$ is a per-layer
threshold. The clip is applied per-layer to attention QK projections and to MoE gate
projections specifically — the modules where logit explosion is observed (Moonshot AI 2025,
§3.1). For most other parameters MuonClip reduces to plain Muon.

The effect on routing dynamics: under Adam-style training at $E = 384$, Moonshot reports
that 5–8% of experts hit the dropped-token threshold within the first 100K steps and stay
dead for the remainder of pretraining (Moonshot AI 2025, Figure 4). Under MuonClip the same
configuration keeps the dead-expert fraction below 1% across the entire run. The aux-loss-free
bias mechanism (which adjusts only at the top-$K$ selection step, not the output expression
— see [aux-loss-free](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/aux-loss-free/)) is what handles the residual
balance once the logits stay in a reasonable range.

**Parameter and FLOP accounting.** Per MoE layer K2 stores
- routed expert weights: $E_r \cdot 3 \cdot d_{model} \cdot d_{ff}$ where each fine-grained
  expert has $d_{ff} \ll d_{ff}^{\text{dense}}$;
- shared expert weights: $1 \cdot 3 \cdot d_{model} \cdot d_{ff}^{(s)}$, typically with
  $d_{ff}^{(s)}$ a small multiple of the routed-expert width;
- gate: $E_r \cdot d_{model}$ for $W_g$ plus $E_r$ scalars for the bias $b$.

The released config (Moonshot AI [Hugging Face card](https://huggingface.co/moonshotai/Kimi-K2-Instruct))
puts $d_{model} = 7168$ and total parameters at $\approx 1.04\text{T}$. Per-token active
parameters are
$1 \cdot d_{ff}^{(s)} \cdot 3 d_{model}$ (shared, always on) plus
$K \cdot d_{ff} \cdot 3 d_{model}$ (routed top-8), summing to $\approx 32\text{B}$. The
attention path uses [MLA](/llm-arch-kb/attention/mla/), the same KV-compressed form as
DeepSeek V2/V3, so the KV cache cost is identical per-token to DeepSeek V3's.

The active-to-total ratio is $32 / 1040 \approx 0.031$ — about half of DeepSeek V3's, meaning
each parameter is exercised at half the rate during a single forward pass. The training-data
implication: K2 needs more total training tokens to thoroughly exercise its parameters than
DeepSeek V3 does at the same total parameter count. Moonshot reports training on $\sim 15.5$T
tokens, which is the rough scale of DeepSeek V3's training corpus.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Reference implementation</p>
## Sigmoid gate with bias and shared expert

```python
def kimi_k2_moe(x, routed_experts, shared_expert, gate, bias, K=8):
    # x: [B, T, d_model]   routed_experts: list of E_r FFNs    gate.weight: [E_r, d_model]
    logits = gate(x)                                # [B, T, E_r]
    scores = logits.sigmoid()                       # [B, T, E_r] in (0, 1) per-expert
    biased = scores + bias[None, None, :]           # selection-time bias (aux-loss-free)
    _, topk_idx = biased.topk(K, dim=-1)            # [B, T, K]
    # Output weights from UNbiased scores — renormalize over the selected K
    topk_scores = scores.gather(-1, topk_idx)       # [B, T, K]
    weights = topk_scores / topk_scores.sum(-1, keepdim=True).clamp(min=1e-9)
    routed = dispatch_and_combine(x, routed_experts, topk_idx, weights)
    return shared_expert(x) + routed
```

The MuonClip optimizer is the harder piece. The clip is applied per-layer to the QK and gate
projections; the rest of the model uses plain Muon (or AdamW for the embeddings). For the
Muon update itself, see the
[Muon reference implementation](https://github.com/KellerJordan/Muon).

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Empirical evidence</p>
## What K2 measures and what it doesn't

**Benchmark headlines.** Moonshot AI 2025 Table 2 reports Kimi K2 reaching DeepSeek-V3.1's
quality on knowledge benchmarks (MMLU 89.5 vs 88.4, GPQA-Diamond 75.1 vs 71.2) and exceeding
it on code/agent benchmarks (LiveCodeBench 53.7 vs 46.2, SWE-Bench Verified 65.8 vs 44.6).
Active parameters: 32B vs DeepSeek V3.1's 37B. The K2-specific gains on agentic tasks are
partly attributed to post-training rather than the MoE structure; the architectural
contribution is matching V3.1's knowledge quality with fewer active parameters.

**Optimizer ablation.** §3.1 of the tech report includes the load-bearing measurement: with
Adam, the QK and gate logits drift to $|\ell| > 10$ within 50K steps of pretraining at this
scale, producing saturating softmax/sigmoid and frozen routing. With Muon alone the drift is
reduced but not eliminated. With MuonClip (Muon + spectral-norm clip at $\tau$ on QK and gate
projections) the drift is bounded throughout the full run. This is the most directly
reproducible claim in the paper — the same optimizer has since been re-used by Moonshot's
[Kimi-Linear](https://huggingface.co/moonshotai/Kimi-Linear-48B-A3B-Instruct) follow-up.

**Routing-distribution diagnostics.** Moonshot AI 2025 Figure 4 plots the per-expert
selection fraction over training. Without MuonClip, the histogram bifurcates into two modes —
a heavy-utilization tail and a dead-expert tail — by step 100K. With MuonClip the distribution
stays near uniform throughout. The aux-loss-free bias is updated with the same schedule as
DeepSeek V3 ($\gamma = 10^{-3}$, sign-only update, per Dai et al. 2024).

**Independent reproductions.** I don't know of a fully independent reproduction of the K2
recipe at 1T parameters — the compute requirements rule out academic replication. Smaller-scale
analogues exist: Kimi-Linear-48B-A3B (Moonshot AI 2025b,
[HF card](https://huggingface.co/moonshotai/Kimi-Linear-48B-A3B-Instruct)) re-uses the
sigmoid-gate, shared-expert, MuonClip combination at $E_r = 256$, and Moonshot reports the
same balanced-routing behavior. This is corroborating but not independent.

**Open question: where does the gain come from?** The paper does not isolate the contribution
of (a) widening $E_r$ from 256 to 384 at fixed activated parameters from (b) switching to
MuonClip from (c) the data and post-training changes. A controlled ablation — Kimi K2 with
$E_r = 256$, K2 with Adam, K2 with the V3.1 post-training mix — would resolve this; no such
ablation has been published.

**Comparison to other wide-pool MoE.**
[Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct) ships $E_r = 512$
routed + 1 shared with top-10 routing at the smaller-model size class. The Qwen team's
release notes do not specify the optimizer; the comparison is suggestive but not directly
informative about whether $E_r = 512$ benefits at K2's total-parameter scale.

**Caveats on closed-model comparisons.** Per the
[knowledge-base closed-model policy](/llm-arch-kb/), Claude / GPT / Gemini-Pro routing details
are not public; this entry makes no claims about their MoE designs.

<Summary>
**Kimi K2 MoE in one breath.** Same shared-expert + fine-grained MoE pattern as DeepSeekMoE,
widened to $E_r = 384$ routed plus 1 shared at 1T total / 32B active, with aux-loss-free
biased top-8 sigmoid gating. The load-bearing engineering contribution is the **MuonClip**
optimizer: Muon plus a per-layer spectral-norm clip on the QK and gate projections, which
keeps the gate logits bounded throughout pretraining and prevents the dead-expert collapse
that would otherwise sink an $E_r = 384$ configuration. Largest publicly documented routed-
expert count in any open release at mid-2025.
</Summary>
