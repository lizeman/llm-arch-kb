<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The third variant

Shazeer's 2020 GLU-variants paper evaluated multiple choices for the gate activation:

- **GLU**: original Dauphin et al. 2017, $\phi = \sigma$ (sigmoid).
- **ReGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{ReLU}$.
- **GeGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{GELU}$.
- **SwiGLU**: $\phi = \mathrm{Swish}$.

All four follow the bilinear form $\mathrm{FFN}(\mathbf{x}) = W_2((W\mathbf{x}) \odot \phi(V\mathbf{x}))$.
The differences are in the gate's smoothness, saturation behavior, and ability to produce hard
zeros.

ReGLU is the variant whose gate produces *hard zeros*: any negative gating input drops the
corresponding output to zero, just like a vanilla ReLU. SwiGLU and GeGLU are smooth (no zeros);
GLU's sigmoid never produces zero but approaches it asymptotically.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The activation</p>
## ReLU gate, bilinear form

$$\mathrm{ReGLU}(\mathbf{x}) = \bigl(W\mathbf{x}\bigr) \odot \mathrm{ReLU}\bigl(V\mathbf{x}\bigr).$$

```python
def reglu_ffn(x, W, V, W2):
    return F.relu(x @ V.T) * (x @ W.T) @ W2.T
```

Same three-projection structure as SwiGLU/GeGLU; the [8/3 · d_model](/llm-arch-kb/ffn-moe/swiglu/)
parameter-matching rule applies identically.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Why nobody ships it</p>
## The hard-zero drawback

In Shazeer (2020) Table 1, ReGLU lands between SwiGLU and GLU on perplexity — slightly worse
than SwiGLU and GeGLU but better than vanilla GLU. The reason it's rarely chosen in production
despite this near-parity is the hard-zero gating: a dead gating unit zeroes out half of the
FFN's effective capacity for that input, with no smooth gradient path back to "alive".

Swish and GELU avoid this failure mode by producing small-but-nonzero outputs for negative
inputs. The cost is slightly more compute per gate; the benefit is no dead units.

For the v1 ship's open-weights models (Llama, OLMo, DeepSeek, Gemma), all four producers chose
SwiGLU or GeGLU. ReGLU shows up occasionally in research baselines but not in production
frontier decoders.

<Summary>
**ReGLU in one breath.** GLU-variant with ReLU as the gate. Functional but lossy: hard zeros

SwiGLU and GeGLU on perplexity, but the dead-unit failure mode makes it the rarely-shipped
third option.
