<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The vanilla FFN

The transformer's feed-forward network has been the same since 2017: two linear layers with an
elementwise nonlinearity between them.

$$\mathrm{FFN}(\mathbf{x}) = W_2\,\phi(W_1\mathbf{x}),$$

where $\phi$ is ReLU in the original paper, GELU in BERT and later. The hidden dim is typically
$4 \cdot d_{\text{model}}$ — most of a transformer's parameters live here.

Two facts motivate replacing it:

1. Gating mechanisms (LSTM, GLU) consistently outperform ungated versions in sequence tasks.
2. The FFN does most of the model's parameter-bound work; even a small per-step quality bump
   compounds.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The gate</p>
## A bilinear, Swish-gated form

GLU (Dauphin et al. 2017) replaces $\phi$ with an elementwise gate: split the projection into
two halves, push one through a sigmoid, multiply. SwiGLU swaps the sigmoid for Swish (a.k.a.
SiLU): $\mathrm{Swish}(z) = z \cdot \sigma(z)$.

$$\mathrm{SwiGLU}(\mathbf{x}) = \bigl(W\mathbf{x}\bigr) \odot \mathrm{Swish}\bigl(V\mathbf{x}\bigr),$$

then a third projection back to $d_{\text{model}}$:

$$\mathrm{FFN}_{\text{SwiGLU}}(\mathbf{x}) = W_2\bigl((W\mathbf{x}) \odot \mathrm{Swish}(V\mathbf{x})\bigr).$$

```python
def swiglu_ffn(x, W, V, W2):
    # x: [..., d_model]
    # W, V: [d_ff, d_model]
    # W2: [d_model, d_ff]
    return (x @ W.T) * silu(x @ V.T) @ W2.T

def silu(z):
    return z * z.sigmoid()
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Parameter accounting</p>
## Why d_ff = 8/3 · d_model

Vanilla FFN has $2 \cdot d_{\text{model}} \cdot d_{\text{ff}}$ parameters. SwiGLU has three
projections, so $3 \cdot d_{\text{model}} \cdot d_{\text{ff}}$. To keep parameter count matched
across the substitution, modern stacks set

$$d_{\text{ff}} = \tfrac{2}{3} \cdot 4 \cdot d_{\text{model}} = \tfrac{8}{3}\,d_{\text{model}}.$$

Llama, PaLM, DeepSeek, and most descendants use this convention. (Some — Gemma — round to a
nearby multiple of a hardware-friendly tile size.)

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

Shazeer (2020) reports SwiGLU beating ReLU FFN by 0.6–1.3 perplexity points on T5-scale pretraining
at matched parameter count (Table 1). The same ordering holds for the related variants GeGLU
(GELU gate) and ReGLU (ReLU gate); SwiGLU and GeGLU tend to lead with very small differences
between them.

Subsequent reports — PaLM, Llama 1, Llama 2, Llama 3 — all confirm SwiGLU as a net win at the
relevant scales. The technique is now the consensus FFN choice for dense decoder-only LLMs.

The cost: a third weight matrix in the FFN means slightly more weight-loading traffic per forward
pass, partially offset by the smaller $d_{\text{ff}}$. In practice the wall-clock difference is
negligible.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Why Shazeer didn't explain it</p>
## "We offer no explanation"

The most quoted line from the SwiGLU paper appears in its conclusion: *"We offer no explanation
as to why these architectures seem to work; we attribute their success, as all else, to divine
benevolence."* The mechanism is empirically robust but theoretically opaque — a recurring pattern
with FFN-side architecture choices.
