<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## Window attention with multiple resolutions

Sliding-window attention attends to the last $W$ keys at full resolution. The receptive field
grows by $W$ per layer. To reach a key $10^6$ tokens away in a 32-layer model with $W = 4096$,
the information has to route through 32 hops — possible but lossy.

LongNet's approach: have different heads attend at different *strides* over the sequence,
combining multiple resolutions of local attention in a single layer. A token can reach a key
$10^6$ tokens away in a *single layer* via the head whose stride covers that distance.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · Dilated attention</p>
## Stride-r local windows per head

For head $i$ with dilation rate $r_i \in \{1, 2, 4, 8, \ldots, 2^k\}$, the head attends to a
window of size $w$ at stride $r_i$. Specifically, query at position $t$ attends to keys

$$\{t - r_i, t - 2 r_i, t - 3 r_i, \ldots, t - w \cdot r_i\}.$$

The head sees $w$ keys, spaced $r_i$ apart, covering a span of $w \cdot r_i$ positions. With
$\log_2(N)$ heads at exponentially-increasing dilation rates, the head ensemble covers
positions from $t$ all the way to $t - N$ at $\log_2(N)$ different resolutions.

```python
def dilated_attention(q, k, v, dilation_rate, window_size):
    # q, k, v: [B, T, d_h]
    # For each query position t, select keys at indices [t - w*r, t - (w-1)*r, ..., t - r]
    T = q.shape[1]
    offsets = torch.arange(1, window_size + 1) * dilation_rate     # [w]
    indices = torch.arange(T)[:, None] - offsets[None, :]          # [T, w]
    indices = indices.clamp(min=0)                                  # causal + boundary
    keys_dilated = k.gather(1, indices.unsqueeze(-1).expand(-1, -1, k.shape[-1]))
    values_dilated = v.gather(1, indices.unsqueeze(-1).expand(-1, -1, v.shape[-1]))
    return scaled_dot_product_attention(q, keys_dilated, values_dilated)
```

Total compute per layer: $O(N \cdot w \cdot \log N)$. With $w \approx 1024$ and the head count
scaling logarithmically in target context length, this scales sub-linearly past softmax's
$O(N^2)$.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Full reachability via composition</p>
## Why log N heads is enough

For any target distance $d \leq N$, there exists a dilation rate $r_i$ such that $d \in
[r_i, w \cdot r_i]$ — the dilation-rate-$r_i$ head's window covers position $t - d$. The head
ensemble therefore has direct access to any past position at *some* resolution, in a single
layer.

The downside: the resolution at distance $d$ is $r_i$ (one key in every $r_i$ positions).
The model attends at fine resolution locally and coarse resolution far away — which is
intuitive but means specific far-away tokens can be skipped.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## What 1B tokens looks like

Ding et al. (Figure 1) report training a transformer on **1 billion-token** sequences end-to-end
via dilated attention. Standard softmax attention OOMs at $10^4$-$10^5$ tokens on the same
hardware; LongNet trains stably to $10^9$ at sub-quadratic cost.

For the practical 32K–1M context regime that production models care about, LongNet's relative
advantage over SWA + global is small — the simpler interleaved-window pattern (Gemma 3, Mistral)
achieves similar receptive-field properties with less implementation complexity. The dilated
attention's value is in the asymptotic regime where the multiplicative reach of $\log N$ heads
genuinely matters.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Where it sits</p>
## Adjacent to but distinct from SWA + global

LongNet shares the "fixed sparse pattern per head" framing with [BigBird](/llm-arch-kb/attention/bigbird/)
and [Sparse Transformer](/llm-arch-kb/attention/sparse-transformer/). The dilation idea — covering
distance via stride rather than window size — is its specific contribution. The combination
of *exponentially-growing strides* across heads is what gives the $O(N \log N)$ scaling rather
than $O(N \sqrt N)$.

No production decoder ships pure LongNet — the asymptotic-regime advantage doesn't translate
to wins at the contexts current models operate in. Worth tracking for very-long-context
applications that haven't yet hit the production frontier.

<Summary>
**LongNet in one breath.** Dilated attention with exponentially-increasing strides across heads.
O(N · log N) compute; full reachability in one layer for any target distance. Demonstrated
training on 1B-token sequences. Below 1M context, simpler SWA + global designs are competitive;
above 1M, LongNet's asymptotic advantage starts to matter.
</Summary>
