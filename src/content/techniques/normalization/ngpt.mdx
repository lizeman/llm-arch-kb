<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## Normalization as a structural property

Standard transformers normalize at a few specific places (Pre-Norm before attention, Pre-Norm
before FFN, final norm before unembedding). Between those normalizations, activation norms
drift — sometimes growing across depth, sometimes shrinking — and the model is asked to learn
weights that produce well-conditioned next-block inputs from poorly-conditioned previous-block
outputs.

Loshchilov et al.'s 2024 proposal: don't ask the model to manage activation norms at all.
Constrain *every* vector in the network — token embeddings, attention K/Q/V projections,
attention output, FFN intermediate, FFN output — to lie on the unit hypersphere. The model
operates entirely on unit vectors; activation norms cannot drift because they are always 1.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The construction</p>
## Project after every operation

Every linear map is followed by an L2 normalization. The block becomes:

$$\mathrm{Norm}(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{\mathbf{x}}{\|\mathbf{x}\|},\qquad \mathrm{Block}(\mathbf{x}) = \mathrm{Norm}\bigl(\mathbf{x} + \alpha_a \cdot (\mathrm{Norm}(\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{x})) - \mathbf{x})\bigr),$$

where $\alpha_a$ is a per-layer learnable scalar controlling how far the residual stream moves
toward the (normalized) attention output. The same construction wraps the FFN sub-layer.

Geometrically: each block defines a target unit vector (the normalized attention output) and
the residual moves a fraction $\alpha$ of the way from the current state toward the target,
then renormalizes back to the sphere. The model lives on the unit sphere and never leaves it.

Weight matrices are also normalized: each row of $W$ is constrained to unit norm, so $W\mathbf{x}$
produces outputs whose components are bounded cosine similarities rather than arbitrary inner
products.

```python
def n_block_attn(x, attn, alpha):
    # x is unit-norm on the hypersphere
    a = attn(x)
    a_unit = a / a.norm(-1, keepdim=True)
    update = a_unit - x
    return (x + alpha * update).renormalize_unit()
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · What it replaces</p>
## No LayerNorm, no RMSNorm

The standard normalization sub-layers (Pre-Norm RMSNorm before attention/FFN) become
unnecessary — every vector is already unit-norm by construction. nGPT removes them entirely.
The architecture has fewer learnable parameters than baseline GPT at matched parameter count
(or equivalently, more "capacity-relevant" parameters at matched total parameter count).

Cosine similarity replaces dot product in attention: $\mathbf{q}\mathbf{k}^\top / \sqrt{d}$
becomes $\cos(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}) \cdot s$ for a learnable temperature $s$, since both
vectors are unit-norm and their dot product *is* their cosine similarity.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

The headline (Table 1 in the paper): **nGPT-1B trains to a target perplexity in 4-20× fewer
steps than equally-sized baseline GPT** across context lengths from 1K to 16K. The speedup is
largest at long contexts; at 1K context the advantage is closer to 4×, while at 16K it is
nearly 20×. The cause the authors propose: the hypersphere constraint regularizes the
optimization landscape, so the loss surface is closer to convex over the operating regime than
the standard architecture's.

The paper is research-stage as of late 2024; no production model has yet shipped using the full
nGPT recipe. The mechanistic claim — that constraining vectors to the sphere makes optimization
easier — is theoretically clean enough to attract follow-up work, and the construction is
mechanically simple.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · What might keep it research-only</p>
## The kernel question

The largest practical obstacle is kernel-level: standard GEMM kernels do not natively support
the per-row weight normalization or the per-step output normalization that nGPT requires. A
production implementation needs custom kernels (or careful fusion of existing primitives) to
avoid leaving the speedup on the table. Until that infrastructure exists at the same maturity
as FlashAttention-3, nGPT's training-step speedup may not translate to wall-clock or dollar
speedup.

Worth tracking. Worth not committing to yet.
