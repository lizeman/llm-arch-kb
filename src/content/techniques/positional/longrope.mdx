<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## YaRN's closed-form is one point in a larger space

YaRN partitions RoPE's $d_h/2$ rotation rates into three regimes — fast (untouched), middle
(smoothly ramped), slow (interpolated) — based on each dimension's rotation count over the
original training window. The boundaries and the ramp shape are derived analytically.

The closed-form recipe is principled but constrained: it forces the rescaling factor to be a
monotone function of dimension index. Empirically, the optimal per-dimension schedule might
not be monotone — some "middle band" dimensions could benefit from being preserved (treated as
fast) and some "fast band" dimensions could benefit from being interpolated (treated as slow).

LongRoPE's bet: search the full $d_h/2$-dimensional space of rescaling factors. If the closed
form is optimal, search recovers it; if it isn't, search finds the better schedule.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The search</p>
## Evolutionary optimization over rescaling factors

Define per-dimension rescaling factors $\lambda_i$, one for each of the $d_h/2$ rotation
frequencies. The scaled rotation rate becomes

$$\theta_i' = \frac{\theta_i}{\lambda_i}.$$

Treat $(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_{d_h/2})$ as a search variable. The objective: minimize
perplexity on a held-out long-context dataset.

Ding et al. use a (1+1) evolutionary strategy with mutations sampled from a Gaussian
perturbation of the previous best schedule. Each candidate $\lambda$ is evaluated by *one
forward pass* over a small validation set — no training is needed during the search, since
RoPE rescaling does not require new weights. The search converges in ~40 generations for the
2M-token target.

```python
def long_rope_search(model, val_data, target_len, init_lambdas, generations=40):
    best = init_lambdas
    best_ppl = eval_ppl(model, val_data, best, target_len)
    for _ in range(generations):
        candidate = mutate(best, sigma=0.1)
        ppl = eval_ppl(model, val_data, candidate, target_len)
        if ppl < best_ppl:
            best, best_ppl = candidate, ppl
    return best
```

The output is a per-dimension schedule that is then frozen during fine-tuning.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · What the search finds</p>
## Non-monotone factors

Ding et al. report (Figure 4) that the discovered $\lambda_i$ schedules are *not* monotone in
$i$. The fast dimensions are mostly untouched (matching YaRN), but the slow-band dimensions
show non-trivial structure — some are rescaled more aggressively than YaRN would predict, some
less. The exact pattern depends on the base model and target length.

This is the paper's empirical claim: the closed-form YaRN schedule is a good *starting point*
but not the optimum for very long extensions. The headroom recovered by search increases as the
extension factor $s = L_{\text{new}} / L_{\text{train}}$ grows past ~8×.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

LongRoPE extends Llama-2 7B from 4K to **2048K (2M)** context after a 1000-step fine-tune,
with PG-19 perplexity at the new length within 0.5 points of the 4K baseline (Ding et al.
Table 5). Beyond ~1M context, LongRoPE outperforms YaRN by 1-2 perplexity points on the same
evaluation; below ~256K, the two are within noise.

The technique requires the search step before fine-tuning, which is a few-hour overhead on the
target hardware. For very-long-context extensions (≥1M) the cost is well-amortized; for the
"merely long" 64K-128K regime, YaRN's closed form remains competitive and simpler.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Why no production decoder uses LongRoPE yet</p>
## Where it fits

Two reasons LongRoPE is not in the v1 model adoption lists:

1. **Most production decoders cap at 128K-1M context.** Llama 3.1 ships 128K via scaled-base
   RoPE; DeepSeek V3 ships 128K via YaRN; Gemma 3 ships 128K via interleaved SWA + global. None
   currently needs the 2M-and-beyond regime where LongRoPE clearly wins.
2. **The search step is awkward to operationalize.** YaRN's closed form is a few lines; LongRoPE
   requires evolutionary search infrastructure that interacts with the validation pipeline.
   When 128K is enough, no one wants the operational complexity.

LongRoPE remains the right tool when the target is genuinely multi-million-token context. As
production demand for that regime grows, the lineage is positioned to be adopted.
