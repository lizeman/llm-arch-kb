<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## PI compresses everything; can we be selective?

[Position Interpolation](/llm-arch-kb/positional/position-interpolation/) (PI) divides every
RoPE rotation rate $\theta_i$ by the extension factor $s$. This solves the extrapolation
problem but hits *every* frequency band equally — including the fast bands that had already
fully spanned the unit circle and didn't need interpolation.

The NTK-aware idea, attributed to an anonymous Reddit user in mid-2023 and later formalized
in the YaRN paper: rescale the base $b$ (not the positions). Because $\theta_i = b^{-2i/d_h}$,
changing $b$ has a frequency-dependent effect — fast bands barely shift, slow bands shift a
lot.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The base rescaling</p>
## Multiply b by s^(d_h / (d_h - 2))

For extension factor $s$ and head dim $d_h$, the NTK-aware new base is:

$$b' = b \cdot s^{d_h / (d_h - 2)}.$$

The exponent is chosen so that the *slowest* dimension (largest $i$, with $\theta_{d_h/2-1} =
b^{-(d_h-2)/d_h}$) has its rotation rate divided by exactly $s$ — matching PI's behavior on
the slow band. The *fast* dimensions ($i$ small, $\theta_i \approx b^0 = 1$) are nearly
untouched because $b'^0 = b^0 = 1$.

The interpolation factor on dimension $i$ becomes

$$\frac{\theta_i'}{\theta_i} = \bigl(\tfrac{b}{b'}\bigr)^{2i/d_h} = s^{-2i/(d_h - 2)},$$

which equals $1$ at $i = 0$ (no change) and equals $1/s$ at $i = d_h/2 - 1$ (full PI rescaling
at the slow end). A smooth frequency-dependent ramp from "untouched" to "interpolated".

```python
def ntk_aware_rope_base(base, extension_factor, d_h):
    return base * extension_factor ** (d_h / (d_h - 2))

def ntk_aware_rope(x, position, base=10000.0, d_h=128, s=4.0):
    b_prime = ntk_aware_rope_base(base, s, d_h)
    theta = b_prime ** (-2 * torch.arange(0, d_h, 2) / d_h)
    angles = position * theta
    cos, sin = angles.cos(), angles.sin()
    x1, x2 = x[..., 0::2], x[..., 1::2]
    return torch.stack([x1 * cos - x2 * sin, x1 * sin + x2 * cos], dim=-1).flatten(-2)
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Why it works without fine-tuning</p>
## The fast bands keep their precision

PI's failure mode at large extensions was the short-range positional acuity loss in the fast
bands. NTK-aware leaves those bands essentially intact — adjacent positions still have nearly
the same relative rotation as before extension. The model's short-range positional reasoning
survives.

What gets interpolated is the slow-band signal — the dimensions that previously rotated by
$\sim 10^{-4}$ radians per position and that the model used for "is this token early or late
in the document" judgments. Compressing those by $s$ is fine because the original signal was
already coarse.

The result: NTK-aware works with *zero fine-tuning* at modest extensions (typically $s \leq 8$).
The model evaluates correctly out of the box. Past $s = 8$, perplexity starts to degrade and
fine-tuning becomes necessary; this is also approximately where YaRN's three-band ramp begins
to outperform.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Where it fits</p>
## The bridge

NTK-aware was originally posted on Reddit by user `bloc97`. The community quickly verified it,
and within months several research groups had formalized and extended the idea. YaRN
(Peng et al. 2023) is the most prominent descendant: it explicitly cites NTK-aware as the
inspiration for the three-band rescaling structure, then adds the smooth ramp between bands
and the attention-logit temperature correction.

For production decoder extensions, the path was: PI (Chen et al. 2023) → NTK-aware (mid-2023)
→ YaRN (Peng et al. 2023) → LongRoPE (Ding et al. 2024). Each fix is downstream of the
previous insight.
