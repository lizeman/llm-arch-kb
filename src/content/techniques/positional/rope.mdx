<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## Position before RoPE

Pre-RoPE transformers encoded position by *adding* something to the token embedding — either a
sinusoidal table (Vaswani et al. 2017) or a learned position table (BERT, GPT-2, GPT-3). Both
approaches have the same shape:

$$\mathbf{x}_t = \mathbf{e}_{\text{tok}(t)} + \mathbf{p}_t,$$

where $\mathbf{p}_t$ is a function of absolute position $t$. The model then has to *learn* to
extract the relative offset $t - s$ between two positions whenever attention needs it.

This works but has two costs: extrapolating beyond the trained position table is brittle, and
the relative-position information is entangled with the token content in the first layer's
input. Several alternatives — relative position embeddings (Shaw et al.), T5-style buckets,
ALiBi — tried to address this by modifying the attention mechanism rather than the input.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The rotation</p>
## Rotate Q and K by position

RoPE encodes position not on the embedding but on the *query and key vectors directly*, by
rotation. Group the head-dim coordinates into pairs $(2i, 2i+1)$. For position $t$, multiply
each pair by a 2D rotation matrix:

$$R_t^{(i)} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos(t\theta_i) & -\sin(t\theta_i) \\ \sin(t\theta_i) & \cos(t\theta_i) \end{pmatrix},\quad
\theta_i = b^{-2i/d_h}, \quad b = 10000.$$

Apply $R_t$ to $W_Q \mathbf{x}_t$ and $W_K \mathbf{x}_t$ before the attention dot product. The
resulting Q and K vectors live on rotating subspaces, with fast rotation in low-index dimensions
and slow rotation in high-index ones.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · Why this gives relative position</p>
## The product collapses

The key identity: for the rotated query at position $m$ and rotated key at position $n$,

$$\bigl(R_m \mathbf{q}\bigr)^\top \bigl(R_n \mathbf{k}\bigr) = \mathbf{q}^\top R_m^\top R_n \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{q}^\top R_{n-m} \mathbf{k}.$$

The inner product depends only on $n - m$. Even though we rotated by absolute positions $m$ and
$n$, attention sees the relative offset $n - m$ alone. The model never needs to learn this
substitution — it is mechanical in the geometry.

```python
def apply_rope(x, theta, position):
    # x: [..., d_h]   pair coordinates: x_pairs = x.view(..., d_h // 2, 2)
    cos = (position * theta).cos()
    sin = (position * theta).sin()
    x1, x2 = x[..., 0::2], x[..., 1::2]
    return torch.stack([x1 * cos - x2 * sin, x1 * sin + x2 * cos], dim=-1).flatten(-2)
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · The frequency ladder</p>
## Fast and slow dimensions

The choice $\theta_i = b^{-2i/d_h}$ with $b = 10000$ gives a geometric ladder of rotation rates:

- $i = 0$: rotates by 1 radian per position. Cycles through the unit circle in 6.3 positions.
- $i = d_h / 2 - 1$: rotates by $\sim 10^{-4}$ radians per position. Barely moves across 10K
  positions.

Low-index dimensions encode *short-range* relative position (any nearby token has a different
phase). High-index dimensions encode *long-range* (the phase only differs at scales of thousands
of tokens). This is the frequency-ladder structure that the figure above shows; it is also what
YaRN exploits to extend the context window — see the [YaRN entry](/llm-arch-kb/positional/yarn/).

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Why it won</p>
## Empirical evidence and adoption

The RoFormer paper (Table 5) reports RoPE matching or beating both sinusoidal and Shaw-style
relative embeddings on translation and language-modeling tasks. The decisive practical wins came
later:

- **No position-table cap.** Sinusoidal extrapolates badly past trained positions; RoPE bases
  cleanly into long-context extensions like YaRN and NTK-aware scaling.
- **Mechanical relative-position math.** Attention gets relative info for free, simplifying
  position-aware reasoning across layers.
- **Tiny implementation footprint.** A few lines of einsum, no extra parameters.

Llama, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek, OLMo, Gemma, and Kimi all use RoPE. The 2024–26 consensus is
unconditional: no major open dense LLM uses anything else.

<Summary>
**RoPE in one breath.** Rotate Q and K coordinate pairs by an angle proportional to position;
inner products then depend only on relative offset. No position table, no extrapolation cliff,
no extra parameters. The default positional encoding for every open decoder since 2022.
</Summary>
