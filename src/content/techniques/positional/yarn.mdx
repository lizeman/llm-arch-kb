<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## RoPE's extrapolation problem

Rotary position embeddings rotate the $i$-th pair of Q and K coordinates by an angle that grows
linearly with position:

$$\theta_i = b^{-2i/d_h},\quad \text{angle}(t, i) = t \cdot \theta_i,$$

with base $b = 10000$ typically. During pretraining, the model sees positions $0$ to $T-1$, so
the *fast* dimensions (small $i$) rotate through the full unit circle many times, while the
*slow* dimensions (large $i$) rotate by only a small amount.

When you ask the same model to process position $t = 10T$ at inference, the slow dimensions
extrapolate far past anything they saw during training. The model has never observed a key with
that rotational phase. Attention degrades.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · Earlier approaches</p>
## PI and NTK-aware

Two extension techniques preceded YaRN:

- **Position Interpolation** (Chen et al. 2023, "Extending Context Window of Large Language
  Models via Position Interpolation"): linearly squeeze new positions into the original range.
  For a $4\times$ extension, position $t$ becomes $t/4$. Every dimension is rescaled equally.
  Works, but loses precision on the fast dimensions because they no longer span the full
  rotational range.

- **NTK-aware scaling** (anonymized "u/bloc97" Reddit post, then formalized in NTK-LongRoPE):
  scale the base $b$ upward rather than squeezing positions. For a $4\times$ extension, set
  $b' = b \cdot 4^{d_h/(d_h-2)}$. The fast dimensions are nearly untouched (good — they already
  span the unit circle); slow dimensions interpolate (good — they avoid extrapolation).

YaRN combines NTK-aware's frequency split with three refinements.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · The three bands</p>
## NTK-by-parts

YaRN partitions RoPE dimensions into three regimes based on each dimension's *rotation count*
over the original training context:

1. **Fast dimensions** that complete $\geq r_{\max}$ rotations over the original window: leave
   their $\theta_i$ untouched. They already saw their full domain.
2. **Slow dimensions** that complete $\leq r_{\min}$ rotations: linearly interpolate to the new
   context length (PI on this band only).
3. **Middle dimensions**: blend between the two regimes via a smooth ramp.

Concretely, with extension factor $s = L_{\text{new}}/L_{\text{train}}$:

$$\theta_i' = \theta_i \cdot \bigl(1 - h_i(s)\bigr) + \frac{\theta_i}{s} \cdot h_i(s),$$

where $h_i(s) \in [0, 1]$ is the ramp that depends on dimension $i$'s rotation count. Fast
dimensions get $h_i = 0$; slow dimensions get $h_i = 1$; middle dimensions get a smooth blend.

```python
def yarn_theta(theta_i, s, rot_count_i, r_min=1, r_max=32):
    if rot_count_i >= r_max:
        return theta_i               # fast band: untouched
    if rot_count_i <= r_min:
        return theta_i / s           # slow band: linearly interpolated
    # middle band: smooth ramp
    h = (rot_count_i - r_min) / (r_max - r_min)
    return theta_i * (1 - h) + (theta_i / s) * h
```

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Temperature scaling</p>
## Adjust softmax sharpness

A longer context means more keys for every query. The softmax distribution naturally flattens;
attention loses sharpness. YaRN compensates by scaling logits by an explicit temperature

$$\text{temp}(s) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 0.1 \log s}},$$

applied multiplicatively to the attention logits. The constants were fit empirically; the form
keeps attention entropy approximately matched to the pretraining context.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Results</p>
## Empirical evidence

Peng et al. (Table 1) report YaRN extending Llama-2 7B's context from 4K to 64K and 128K via a
short 400-step fine-tune (~0.1% of pretraining tokens), with perplexity strictly below PI,
NTK-aware, and dynamic NTK on PG-19 and Proof-Pile at all tested lengths.

DeepSeek-V2 and V3 (Liu et al. 2024; Liu et al. 2024) report using YaRN for the 4K-to-128K
extension, citing its sample efficiency vs continued pretraining with a uniformly scaled base.

For models like Llama 3.1, the team went with a simpler scaled-base recipe (multiplying $b$ by
a fixed factor) plus long continued pretraining, which works at the larger token budget but
trades sample efficiency for simplicity.

<Summary>
**YaRN in one breath.** Frequency-aware RoPE rescaling: preserve fast rotations, interpolate
slow ones, smooth between. Add a small temperature correction to keep softmax sharpness
constant. 32× context extension on Llama-2 in 400 steps; adopted by DeepSeek V2/V3 for 128K.
</Summary>
