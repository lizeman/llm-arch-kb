<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 1 · Premise</p>
## The pre-residual era

Before ResNet, networks deeper than ~20 layers were intractable to train. Gradients either
vanished (multiplicative attenuation through nonlinearities) or exploded (amplification through
unbounded weight matrices). VGG topped out at 19 layers.

The diagnosis from He et al. (2015) was sharp: depth itself was not the problem. The problem
was that deep stacks were being asked to *transform* activations through a long chain of
nonlinear functions, when most of those layers should be doing very little — close to the
identity.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 2 · The fix</p>
## Add, don't replace

Reformulate each block to learn a *residual function* on top of an identity skip:

$$\mathbf{x}_{\ell+1} = \mathbf{x}_\ell + f_\ell(\mathbf{x}_\ell).$$

Now $f_\ell$ only needs to learn what is different about $\mathbf{x}_{\ell+1}$ vs
$\mathbf{x}_\ell$. The "do nothing" baseline is $f_\ell \equiv 0$, which is reachable with a
simple bias initialization. Depth becomes nearly free.

The gradient with respect to a deep input is

$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{x}_\ell} = \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{x}_{L}}\;\prod_{k=\ell}^{L-1}\Bigl(I + \frac{\partial f_k}{\partial \mathbf{x}_k}\Bigr).$$

The identity term inside each product keeps the gradient from collapsing to zero or blowing up,
even at depths of hundreds of layers.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 3 · The residual stream</p>
## A useful mental model

A modern transformer block has two sub-layers (attention, FFN), each with its own residual:

```python
def block(x, attn, ffn, norm1, norm2):
    x = x + attn(norm1(x))   # attention adds a delta
    x = x + ffn(norm2(x))    # FFN adds another delta
    return x
```

Think of $\mathbf{x}$ as a flowing *stream* whose dimensionality $d_{\text{model}}$ is fixed
across depth. Every block reads from the stream (via the norm + sublayer), computes a
correction, and writes that correction back additively. Nothing is replaced; nothing is lost
unless explicitly written over.

This framing has become the standard mechanistic-interpretability lens (Elhage et al. 2021,
"A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits"). Each attention head and FFN neuron is a
read/write operation against the residual stream. The stream is the shared memory.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 4 · Geometry across depth</p>
## The norm grows

A consequence of pure Pre-Norm residual layout: the residual stream's norm tends to grow
monotonically with depth, because each block adds a perturbation but nothing trims it. A 100-layer
model can have a stream norm 10× larger at the output than at the embedding.

This is mostly fine — the final RMSNorm before the unembedding normalizes it back — but it has
two effects worth knowing:

1. **Layers deep in the network see a different distribution than shallow ones.** Some recent
   designs (DeepNorm, OLMo 2) deliberately rein in the growth via initialization or by reverting
   to Post-Norm with care.
2. **Sandwich placements** (Gemma 3's norm-everywhere) wrap each residual branch in normalization,
   capping the magnitude of the additive correction per block. See the
   [norm-placement entry](/llm-arch-kb/normalization/norm-placement/) for the placement zoo.

<p class="section-eyebrow">§ 5 · Variations on the basic recipe</p>
## What changes the residual itself

The standard $\mathbf{x} + f(\mathbf{x})$ is the consensus choice for every open frontier
decoder. A few research directions worth flagging for later entries:

- **ReZero** (Bachlechner et al. 2020): scale each residual branch by a learnable scalar
  initialized to zero. Trains stably without warmup or norm.
- **DeepNet** (Wang et al. 2022): rescale the residual additively to stabilize 1000+ layer
  transformers.
- **Hyper-Connections** (Zhu et al. 2024): replace the single residual stream with multiple
  parallel streams that exchange information via learned read/write matrices.

These are roadmap entries for the v2 and v3 ships. For v1, "the residual" means the original
ResNet formulation, applied twice per transformer block, on either side of the norm depending
on placement.

<Summary>
**Residual in one breath.** Each block adds a learned perturbation to a clean linear stream
instead of transforming activations in place. Gradients flow through the identity term, depth
becomes nearly free, and the residual stream itself becomes the shared substrate that
interpretability research now treats as the model's working memory.
</Summary>
